The other day I was watching a friend of mine, who’s a film producer, do some work on her 13" Macbook Pro. She was working on the budget, the shooting schedule, arrangements for transport and accommodation, and reviewing hundreds of photos from a location scout. She was switching between four or five different apps, each suited to a different kind of data. And all the files for this project were organised under a folder for this project, which sat alongside folders for several other projects she is also working on.

These to me are essentials of a pro computing environment:

  • I can organise data files by the work I am doing with them, instead of by their type or by the app that created them.
  • I can use whatever app best suits a particular task to do it, regardless of which company made the app.
  • I can use several such apps at once to achieve my work, instead of expecting one megalithic app to handle all tasks equally well.

As long as iOS remains tied down by app-specific (or even app-company-specific) silos and cloud containers, no iPad whatever its size will deserve the name “Pro”.
