The Perfect Puzzle

Monkey Island: insult sword fighting. Funnu, engagin, but most of all: taught you a skill, then made you use it in a different way – a twist Portal: using portals, twisting the way you think. Hole in the ceiling to come out the floor. This is the key to puzzles, why puzzles are entertaining. It’s not about thinking outside the box, it’s about training in a skill, then adapting that skill to a new problem: analysis and problem solving. Intellectual skills that exercise the brain,something innately enjoyable.

The perfect puzzle has two requirements:

  • it must be fun to fail.
  • it must come with a twist.

quotes from http://www.adventureclassicgaming.com/index.php/site/interviews/515/

Q “We’ll blame them than if that ever happens. So some other Monkey Island conventions… Is insult swordfighting going to be back?”

A “Not exactly. But there’s stuff that is in the vein of insult swordfighting. There are references to insult swordfighting. It’s also a challenge. Can it be good enough to be good by itself? Can it be good enough to where if you never played a Monkey Island game you’ll think it’s awesome? That’s one side. And then if that’s true, can it also be so good that lives up to the old stuff for the old fans? It’s a real challenge. All the callbacks in general are like, “I’m Guybrush Threepwood, Mighty Pirate™!” It worked in text, and as you start to hear it, I start to joke about it, “I’m Guybrush Threepwood!” And it’s like even I’m getting sick of hearing it when Guybrush says to himself, “Oh, god!” Those callbacks are interesting on how to balance. There are mechanical things like insult swordfighting that are piratey, and you’re in the world aggregating abilities like, “Ok, I can learn how to do this, learn how to do this from over here.” You’re kind of grinding for that agency the way you do in insult swordfighting, and there’s references to it. But right now, as is planned, we’re not going to be doing insult swordfighting exactly like you did in Monkey Island 1. But I think if you’re a fan of insult swordfighting and you’re waiting for it, you’re going to be satisfied in both the experience and the references.”

Q “For me, my favorite part of it was not so much the grinding to collect all your insults and comebacks, but the part where you’ve learned all the comebacks, and then you get thrown a new set of insults and you’ve got to rematch them, like the sword master and the battles there. Do you have that same kind of creativity applied?”

A “Just conventionally, that’s what I like to do all the way through. It’s like you’ve learned how to use this in a clever way, now you have used it in a totally opposite way. Wallace and Gromit episode 3 does that, where it’s like, “This dog digs, this dog steals things. Get them to subvert their powers. And now, an act and a half later, use their powers in a totally different way with the things I’ve presented you.” That’s just good design, that’s why insult swordfighting is not just something you remember as being funny but good design.”
